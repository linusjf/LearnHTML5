Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar

The Father of the Indian Constitution

Here's a timeline of Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar's life:

  • 1891 - Born on 14th April in the town and military cantonment of Mhow (now officially known as Dr Ambedkar Nagar in Madhya Pradesh) and named Bhimrao. His parents were of the Mahar (dalit) caste, treated as untouchables and subjected to socio-economic discrimination. His father, Ramji Maloji Sakpal, served in the British army as a Subedar. Babasaheb was his 14th and last child. His mother's name was Bhimabai.
  • 1896 - Family moved to Satara and children were schooled there. His father registered his name as Ambadawekar which means he comes from his native village. Ambadawe. His Marathi Brahmin teacher, Krishnaji Keshav Ambedkar, changed his surname from 'Ambadawekar' to his own surname 'Ambedkar' in school records.
  • 1897 - Family moved to Bombay and Ambedkar enrolled at Elphinstone High School as the sole untouchable.
  • 1906 - Married at 15 to a nine-year-old girl, Ramabai.
  • 1907 - Passed his matriculation exam.
  • 1908 - Joined Elphinstone College.
  • 1912 - Obtained his degree in Economics and Political Science from Bombay University.
  • 1915 - Passed M.A. exam from Columbia University majoring in Economics. Passed a second M.A. the next year.
  • 1923 - Completed D.Sc in Economics from University of London. Called to the bar by Gray's Inn.
  • 1927 - Received Ph.D degree in Economics from Columbia University. Launched movement against untouchability. Burnt copies of Manusmriti on 25th December.
  • 1932 - Signed the Poona Pact securing reserved seats for the Depressed Classes.
  • 1935 - Appointed principal of Government Law College, Bombay. Wife Ramabai expired after long illness.
  • 1936 - Founded Independent Labour Party. Published book Annihilation of Caste.
  • 1940 - After Lahore Resolution, published tract Thoughts on Pakistan arguing that Hindus should concede Pakistan to Muslims.
  • 1948 - Married Sharada Kabir who adopted the name Savita Ambedkar.
  • 1949 - Completed final draft of Constitution.
  • 1951 - Resigned as Law Minister when Hindu code bill defeated in Parliament.
  • 1956 - Converted to Buddhism. Died on 6th December in his sleep.
